Value Proposition

From my competitive analysis of existing fuel-finding apps, I identified gaps in real-time pricing, route optimization, and user experience â€” leading to the following value propositions:

1.Real-Time Pricing

Unlike traditional gas station finders that show outdated prices, GasRoute provides live pricing data that helps drivers make informed decisions and save money on fuel costs.

2. Route Optimization

GasRoute considers your current route and traffic conditions to suggest the most efficient gas stations, minimizing detours and travel time while maximizing fuel savings.

3. One-Tap Navigation

Seamless integration with navigation apps allows drivers to select a gas station and start navigation with a single tap, reducing distraction and improving safety while driving.

4. Smart Decision Making

Clear visual indicators and cost-per-mile calculations help drivers quickly compare options and make the best choice based on their priorities: time, cost, or convenience.

Design Decisions

To make GasRoute not only visually appealing but also usable and safe for drivers, I established a set of design decisions and content guidelines. These were informed by both primary research (user interviews & surveys) and secondary research (best practices & accessibility standards).

Task-first navigation: Prioritize the primary task of finding gas stations with minimal cognitive load and distraction.

Large touch targets: Ensure all interactive elements are easily tappable while driving, with adequate spacing and visual feedback.

Clear visual hierarchy: Use typography, color, and spacing to guide attention to the most important information first.

One-tap actions: Minimize the number of steps required to complete common tasks like navigation and price comparison.

Real-time data: Display live pricing and availability information to build trust and ensure accuracy.

Safety-first design: Implement voice commands, large text, and minimal text input to reduce driver distraction.

Accessibility

Driver Safety

For this project, I ensured accessibility by designing for driver safety and minimizing cognitive load. The interface prioritizes quick scanning and one-tap actions to reduce distraction while driving.

  1. Implemented large touch targets (minimum 44px) for easy interaction while driving.
  2. Used high contrast colors and clear typography for visibility in various lighting conditions.
  3. Designed voice command integration for hands-free operation and reduced visual distraction.

Offline Capabilities

I designed offline capabilities to support users in areas with poor connectivity. The app caches essential data and provides basic functionality even without internet access. This ensures reliability and accessibility for all users, regardless of their location or network conditions.

Reflections

The GasRoute project taught me that designing for drivers requires a unique approach that prioritizes safety and minimal cognitive load. Working with real-time data and navigation integration showed how technical constraints can drive innovative design solutions. I also learned how important it is to test with users in realistic driving scenarios to ensure the interface doesn't create dangerous distractions. Most importantly, this project reinforced the value of task-first design and the importance of reducing steps in critical user flows.
