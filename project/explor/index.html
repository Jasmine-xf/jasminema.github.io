Design Decisions

To make ExplrEcho not only visually appealing but also usable and inclusive, I established a set of design decisions and content guidelines. These were informed by both primary research (user interviews & surveys) and secondary research (best practices & accessibility standards).

Minimalist layouts: Reduce cognitive overload and keep attention on core travel stories.

Strong contrast: Consistent use of typography, spacing, and image placement for effortless scanning.

Prominent CTAs: Emphasize actions like Publish, Save, and Explore, supporting intuitive flows.

Consistent components: Cards, buttons, and nav bars follow reusable system to maintain coherence.

Visual hierarchy: Clear typography & spacing for easy scan

Dark Mode: Designed for users who browse or write at night, reducing eye strain and aligning with the cinematic, immersive feel of travel storytelling.

Accessibility: Colour Contrast

For this project, I applied a colour contrast grid to make sure every element remained accessible and easy to read. I tested all chosen hex codes against WCAG standards, compared the results directly with my layouts, and iterated by replacing low-contrast values with sharper alternatives, ensuring both light and dark modes delivered clear readability without sacrificing the immersive aesthetic.

Reflections

Key findings revealed that 78% of travelers struggle with generic recommendations that don't match their preferences. Users expressed frustration with time spent filtering irrelevant suggestions and missing out on hidden gems. The research identified three distinct traveler personas: Budget Backpackers, Luxury Seekers, and Cultural Explorers.
